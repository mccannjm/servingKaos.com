Journalism

Every investigative segment follows the same structure.

Intent "Here's what they say they're doing" Reality "Here's what's actually happening" Gap "Here's the space between those two things" Cross The clip that makes you laugh because you can see the gap

John Oliver has been doing this decomposition every week for a decade.
He just doesn't have notation for it.

Race Engineering

Every stint on track is the same three questions.

Setup What the driver wants the car to do Stint What the car is actually doing — the telemetry Analysis The gap between setup and stint. Where the time is.

The RCEgram — the crew chief's diagnostic.
Same three rings. Different domain.

Medicine

Every patient encounter is the same decomposition.

Chief Complaint What the patient wants — why they came in Assessment What the clinician finds — labs, vitals, exam Plan What's unresolved — the treatment to bridge the gap

SOAP notes. Taught in every medical school.
The structure was always there.

Logistics

Every shipment is the same three things.

Order What the customer wants — where and when Position Where the goods actually are right now ETA Delta The gap between promise and position. The delay. The reroute.

If the system knew intent from reality from gap,
you'd stop taking the Kansas detour.

Software

Every bug report. Every feature request. Every standup.

Expected What the code should do Actual What the code actually does Steps to Reproduce The gap — the path from one to the other

Every bug template in the world
is a three-ring decomposition.
